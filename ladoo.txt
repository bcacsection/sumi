<h3>Ladoo</h3>
<p><font size="4">
<u>Ingredients</u><br>
<p><font size="4">	
<ul><b>For making Boondi</b><li>
1 cup Chickpea flour (Gram flour/Besan)</li><li>
A pinch of Baking soda</li><li>
½ cup water (adjust as required)</li><li>
<b>For the Sugar syrup</b></li><li>
1 cup Sugar</li><li>
½ cup water</li><li>
<b>Other Ingredients:</b></li><li>
1 teaspoon Cardamom powder</li><li>
A pinch of Edible Camphor (Pacha Karpooram)</li><li>
A pinch of Nutmeg powder</li><li>
10 to 15 cashews, broken to small pieces and fried till light brown in 1 teaspoon of butter orghee</li><li>
10 to 15 Raisins (optional), fried as above</li><li>
Required oil for deep-frying</li><li>
</ul>

 
</font></p>
<h3><u>Directions</u></h3>
<p><font size="4">
<b>1.	Making Boondi:</b><br>
2.	Sieve chickpea flour and baking soda in a sifter.<br>
3.	Add in water slowly and make it into a smooth batter.<br>
4.	The batter should be little thick, not runny consistency. Make the batter perfectly, only then you will get round boondi.<br>
5.	Now heat oil over medium heat for deep-frying boodi.<br>
6.	Now take a perforated ladle as shown in the picture below. Hold it over oil. Pour a ladleful of batter over the ladle and spread it out. The boondis will fall drop by drop.<br>
7.	Fry the boondi till they are little soft. The boondi shouldn’t become crisp. Drain them and set aside.<br>
<b>8.	Making the Sugar Syrup:</b><br>
9.	Take sugar and water in a deep pan and mix well.<br>
10.	Bring it to a boil and cook it to a single string stage. <br>When you take the spoon from the syrup and see, the last drop falling from the syrup should form a single string like consistency. For detail check above in the post.<br>
11.	At this point, turn off the heat and add the boondi to the syrup.<br>
<b>12.	Making Traditional Boondi Laddoo:</b><br>
13.	Mix well and then add cardamom powder and a pinch of edible camphor and nutmeg powder. Just make sure you add very very tiny bit of edible camphor, otherwise you get a bitter taste. Set this aside.<br>
14.	Add the fried cashews to the sugar coated boondi and mix well. Or you can also add each cashew while making the laddoo and then shape them to Traditional Boondi Laddoo.<br>
15.	Take a small portion of this mix and shape it into a tight laddoo.<br>
16.	Let them cool and store in an airtight jar for about a week.<br>


</font>
</p>
